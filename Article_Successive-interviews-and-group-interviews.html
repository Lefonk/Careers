Successive interviews and group interviews

WHAT ARE THESE INTERVIEWS FOR?

At the bank, the applicant selection process often begins with a group session, combining group interviews and successive interviews.
What is expected from you and what should you do to succeed in this type of interview?

  • Successive interviews: these mean you meet all the relevant interviewers on the same day: HR, direct superior, L+2, work colleagues. The applicant may see these interviewers individually or all at the same time.
  • Group interviews: these are used for jobs calling for relational qualities (advice, sales, negotiation, speaking, team work and so on). They consist of putting applicants in situations close to the reality of the job to see how they express themselves and interact. The objective is to evaluate personality and relational capacity: ability to listen, verbal expression, capacity for analysis and summary, leadership and so on.

WHAT SHOULD YOU EXPECT FROM THIS KIND OF INTERVIEW?

Although you can prepare for these meetings as you would for a traditional interview, they are run differently.

  • Successive interviews: whilst remaining consistent, the applicant needs to adapt his responses to each interviewer, as each interviewer will have specific expectations. The HR manager will be interested in personality, whilst the operational manager will validate technical knowledge and how this knowledge is applied. It is up to the applicant to ask questions about the everyday reality of the job from his future colleagues and questions about the company's overall policy at L+2.
  • Collective interviews: these usually start with a quick presentation of the company and the job. Next, after going around the table, the applicants are put in certain situations: a more detailed verbal presentation, individual or group exercises (discussions/debates, simulations of professional interviews, preparation of pitches and so on). This interview is sometimes followed by a debrief with a recruiter, which is an opportunity to get constructive feedback about your application and how you come across in a group. The group interview is used for pre-selection, so avoid being passive, smile and take an interest in the other applicants: the aim is to make the recruiters want to see you in an individual interview.

WHAT TO DO

  • Adapt to each interviewer.
  • Be consistent in what you say from one interview to the next.
  • Make a note of the names and positions of the different interviewers.
  • In a group interview, listen to other people to understand their ideas and arguments.
  • Also make sure that you remain polite and diplomatic, even if they have a different point of view.

WHAT NOT TO DO

  • Interrupt the recruiters or the other applicants.
  • Put yourself forward too much during the group interview.
  • Think too quickly that you have been successful: even if the L+2 does not interview you, there may well be other applicants waiting.
  • Look only at the interviewer asking you a question: you need to consider everyone present.
